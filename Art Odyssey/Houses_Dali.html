Welcome to Dalí's House

Salvador Felipe Jacinto Dalí Domènech was born May 11, 1904, in the Catalonian town of Figueres in northeastern Spain. Known for his theatrical behavior and appearance (especially his cartoonishly waxed mustache) and for paintings (frenzied swirls of delirious, if not demented, subject matter) and found-object sculptures (e.g., Lobster Telephone, 1938, a handset sheathed in a crustacean carapace), Dalí became synonymous with Surrealism. He made paintings, sculptures and films about the dreams he had. He painted melting clocks and floating eyes, clouds that look like faces and rocks that look like bodies. At 25, he met his future wife and lifelong companion, Gala, at his family’s vacation home in Cadaqués.

― HISTORY

Salvador Dalí's House in Portlligat, Girona, Spain, provides an intriguing insight into the life and creative genius of one of the 20th century's most famous surrealist painters. Dalí bought the house in 1930 and spent more than four decades turning it into a place that reflected his unique personality and imaginative spirit. It wasn't just where he lived; it was his main workspace where he found inspiration from the beautiful surroundings to bring his surreal ideas to life on canvas. The home was his main residence between 1930 to 1982 till the death of his wife where he resided elsewhere.

Besides being an architectural and artistic gem, Dalí's house also gives us a glimpse into his personal life and relationships. Visitors can learn about his marriage to Gala, who was not only his wife but also his muse and collaborator. They can also discover more about his connections with other influential figures in the surrealist movement. And with a collection of personal items and memorabilia, the house offers a touching peek into the everyday life of one of the most well-known artists of the 20th century.

― MODERN DAY

After Salvador Dalí's passing in 1989, the Gala-Salvador Dalí Foundation took ownership of his house in Portlligat, recognizing its cultural and historical significance. Committed to preserving Dalí's artistic legacy, the foundation embarked on an extensive restoration project to maintain the house's original splendour. Collaborating with preservationists and art historians, they meticulously conserved the unique architectural features and whimsical decor that characterised Dalí's former residence, ensuring that every aspect of the house reflected the artist's eccentric spirit.

Beyond mere preservation, the Gala-Salvador Dalí Foundation transformed the house into a dynamic tribute to the artist's life and work. Educational programs were established, inviting visitors to delve into Dalí's creative universe through guided tours and interactive exhibits. Visitors are guided through the intimate spaces of Dalí's bedroom, the grandeur of his expansive studio where masterpieces were born, and the curious artefacts of his cabinet, each room offering insight into the artist's enigmatic personality and artistic process. The exterior showcases breathtaking views and coastlines of the ocean that were once walked by Dalí himself. Salvador Dalí's house stands as a living testament to his enduring influence and artistic legacy.

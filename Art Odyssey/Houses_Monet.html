Welcome to Monet's House

A luminary of the impressionist movement, Claude Monet was born on November 14th 1840 in Paris France. He's celebrated for his revolutionary artistic style in light and nature. Producing iconic series of paintings like "Water Lillies," "Rouen Cathedral," and "Haystacks," he reshaped the world's perception of beauty and innovation. While his life was filled with numerous personal tragedies and health challenges, his passion for painting persevered until the very end. His legacy is one that continues to inspire generations of artists around the world.

― HISTORY

Claude Monet’s Giverny House was home to the acclaimed artist from 1883 to his death in 1926 and remains a pivotal place in art history. Seeking solace from Paris, Monet settled in the Normandy region of France, acquiring property in the quaint village of Giverny. He leased the property in 1890 and eventually acquired the land with the ambitious endeavour of designing the home to be a living masterpiece. Meticulous in nature, he transformed the house, surrounding gardens with a keen eye for detail. A central part of his vision was creating a water garden inspired by a Japanese styled bridge adorned with cascading wisterias surrounded by his beloved water lilies. Here he painted numerous impressionist paintings of his home, many of which are now featured in Musée Marmottan Monet in Paris.

Following his passing, the house remained a private residence attended by his family. However Michel Monet, the second son of Claude Monet died heirless in 1966 and bequeathed the estate to Académie des beaux-arts to ensure its stewardship and long term preservation. From 1977, a series of renovations are attributed to Gérald Van der Kemp who curated for the Palace of Versaille. Due to his efforts, restorations were able to progress in repairing many of the damages within the estate. Established in 1980 upon the public declaration of the estate, the Fondation Claude Monet swiftly gained prominence, drawing a multitude of visitors, both domestic and international, from April to November each year.

― MODERN DAY

Monet’s Giverny house underwent several expansions in 1998 with the addition of contemporary new wings adjacent to the original structure. To enhance the space, further upgrades took place from 2022 to 2023 to augment the exhibition by an additional 30%. With the goal of preserving this house as a cultural site, all layouts and design remain faithful to the original architecture, preserving historical authenticity and design.

Visitors can visit Monet’s Giverny house and tour the famous artist’s private sanctuaries from his characteristic quaint rooms to his expansive studio where many of his well known masterpieces were painted. Charm can be found at every corner from the eclectic array of colours and furniture, all which display period artefacts and sculptures that are selected by Monet’s contemporaries to honour his legacy. Among the many rooms, some of his most famous include the Blue Lounge which was a sitting room for the children, Claude Monet’s bedroom and the bright yellow dining room which clashed with Victorian fashion of the time. The museum showcases all of his prints and also provides insight on the techniques he expertly employed to create his best pieces.

As visitors embark on a journey through Monet's Giverny house, they are enveloped in an immersive experience that intertwines art, history, and the serene beauty of the surrounding landscape just as Monet envisioned.
